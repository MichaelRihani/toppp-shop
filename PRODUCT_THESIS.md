# Product Thesis

## One Sentence

Toppp helps shoppers find the best product faster by turning noisy product research into three trustworthy picks: budget, best for most people, and upgrade.

## Problem

Most product searches create more work than confidence.

Shoppers often bounce between:

- Amazon search results
- Google results
- Sponsored listicles
- Reddit threads
- YouTube reviews
- AI assistant summaries
- Manufacturer pages

Each source can be useful, but the buyer still has to reconcile ratings, price, specs, availability, review quality, and personal fit.

Toppp is built for the moment when someone thinks:

> I need to buy this thing. I do not want to spend an hour researching it. I also do not want to blindly trust the first result.

## Product Bet

For many everyday purchases, people do not need infinite choice. They need a confident shortlist.

The best shopping interface for these moments is not a marketplace grid. It is an answer:

- The budget pick
- The pick for most people
- The upgrade pick

That structure is simple enough for busy shoppers, but still respects meaningful differences in price sensitivity and product quality.

## Target User

The first target user is a busy household buyer.

They:

- Buy practical products often.
- Want a shortcut.
- Prefer clear tradeoffs over endless browsing.
- Are skeptical of sponsored rankings and inflated reviews.
- Often prefer Amazon or major retailers for convenience.
- Still care whether the recommendation is actually good.

## Core Experience

1. User searches a product, such as `robot vacuum` or `USB-C cable`.
2. Toppp maps the query to a researched product page if one exists.
3. The page shows three recommendation cards.
4. Each card includes product image, price, retailer, pros, cons, and a clear CTA.
5. If Toppp has not researched the product, it says so and suggests live alternatives.

## Why Three Picks

Three picks match how many people naturally buy:

- "What is the cheapest good one?"
- "What should most people buy?"
- "What is the better one if I want to spend more?"

Toppp uses those buckets to make the answer feel complete without becoming overwhelming.

## What Makes This Different

Toppp is not trying to be:

- A general marketplace
- A review blog
- A chatbot
- A sponsored affiliate listicle
- A price comparison engine only

Toppp is an opinionated decision layer.

The product is successful when the user feels:

> I understand the tradeoff, I trust the pick, and I can buy now.
