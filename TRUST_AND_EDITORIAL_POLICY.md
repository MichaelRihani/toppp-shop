# Trust And Editorial Policy

Trust is the product.

Toppp should only succeed if users believe the recommendations are not for sale.

## Ranking Rules

- No sponsored rankings.
- No ads.
- Affiliate links never change the winner.
- Toppp can recommend a product even when there is no affiliate relationship.
- Every live recommendation must include real tradeoffs.
- Unsupported products should not generate fake winners.

## Affiliate Disclosure

Toppp pages should clearly disclose affiliate relationships.

Suggested disclosure:

> This page contains affiliate links. If you buy through these links, we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. As an Amazon Associate I earn from qualifying purchases.

## Editorial Standard

Before a product category becomes live, it should have:

- A defined product scope
- Multiple candidate products
- Current price and availability checks
- Independent evidence sources where available
- Specs or manufacturer data where relevant
- Three pros and three cons per winner
- A backup product per bucket
- Human approval before publication

## AI's Role

AI can help with:

- Query mapping
- Candidate discovery
- Deduplicating product variants
- Summarizing reviews and specifications
- Drafting pros, cons, and tradeoff language
- Creating structured product records

AI should not independently publish final winners.

## Human Role

Humans approve:

- Category scope
- Source quality
- Winner selection
- Final language
- Any claim about testing, ratings, review counts, or retailer eligibility

## Claim Discipline

Toppp should avoid claims that cannot be proven.

Examples:

- Do not claim Prime eligibility unless verified through an approved source.
- Do not claim hands-on testing unless the item was actually tested.
- Do not claim a product is the best on Amazon unless the methodology supports that claim.
- Do not cite raw review content unless the source allows that use.

## Unsupported Searches

When Toppp has not researched a product, the honest response is:

> We have not researched this yet.

This is a core trust feature, not a failure state.
